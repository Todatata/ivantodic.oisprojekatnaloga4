LOUVRE HISTORY

  1. Medieval and Renaissance Period (12th-16th centuries)
    • 1190: Construction of the original fortress ordered by King Philip II (Philip Augustus).
    • 1546: Transformation into a royal Renaissance residence under King Francis I, who also acquired the Mona Lisa.
    • 1572-1610: Expansion continues with Catherine de' Medici commissioning the Tuileries Palace and Henry IV building the Grande Galerie.
  2. Revolutionary and Napoleonic Era (18th-19th centuries)
    • 1793: The Louvre opens as a public museum following the French Revolution.
    • 1803-1815: Under Napoleon Bonaparte, it becomes the Musée Napoléon and expands with artworks taken from across Europe.
    • 1852-1870: Napoleon III completes major architectural expansions, linking the Louvre and Tuileries Palaces.
  3. Modern Period (20th-21st centuries)
    • 1940-1945: Artworks are evacuated and hidden during World War II to protect them from Nazi looting.
    • 1983-1989: President François Mitterrand's Grand Louvre project leads to the construction of I. M. Pei's glass pyramid.
    • 2012-present: Louvre expands globally with the opening of Louvre-Lens (2012) and Louvre Abu Dhabi (2017), while digitizing its vast collection.
