The Louvre[a] or the Louvre Museum (French: Musée du Louvre), is a national art museum in Paris, France, and one of the most famous museums in the world.


About:

  • The museum is housed in the Louvre Palace (Palais du Louvre), originally built as a fortress in the late 12th to 13th century under Philip II .

  • Remnants of the fortress are visible in the basement of the museum. The building was extended many times to form the present Louvre Palace. In 1682, Louis XIV chose the Palace of Versailles for his household, leaving the Louvre primarily as a place to display the royal collection, including, from 1692, a collection of ancient Greek and Roman sculpture. In 1692, the building was occupied by the Académie des Inscriptions et Belles-Lettres and the Académie royale de peinture et de sculpture, which in 1699 held the first of a series of salons.

  • The Académie remained at the Louvre for 100 years. During the French Revolution, the National Assembly decreed that the Louvre should be used as a museum to display the nation's masterpieces.

Tickets

Tickets can be used to access both the permanent collections and temporary exhibitions. Time-slot bookings are recommended, including for free-admission visitors.

Prices:

  1. Full price: 22€
  2. Reduced price: 12€
  3. Free: for under 18, EU residents under 26, disabled persons and their accompanying person, job seekers, and others.

Free admission on the first Saturday of each month from 6 p.m. to 9:45 p.m. and on 14 July.

Tickets can be purchased online, at the museum ticket counters, or at various tourist information points in Paris.

Visitors with a ticket can enter the museum through the main entrance, the Carrousel du Louvre shopping mall, or the Porte des Lions entrance.

It is recommended to book tickets in advance, especially during peak tourist seasons, to avoid long lines at the entrance.

For more information and to purchase tickets, visit the official Louvre page.
